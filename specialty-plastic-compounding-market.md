<p><p>The specialty plastic compounding market features high-density polyethylene (HDPE) and low-density polyethylene (LDPE) as primary types. HDPE is known for its strength, chemical resistance, and suitability for various applications like containers and piping. In contrast, LDPE is more flexible, provides excellent low-temperature performance, and is commonly used in films and packaging. Both types are tailored through compounding processes to enhance properties, making them ideal for specialized products across industries such as packaging, automotive, and consumer goods.</p></p>
