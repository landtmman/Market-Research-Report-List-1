<p><p>The flame retardant market for lithium batteries can be categorized into two main types: organic and inorganic flame retardants. Organic flame retardants, derived from carbon-based compounds, typically enhance thermal stability and reduce flammability through chemical reactions during combustion. In contrast, inorganic flame retardants, composed of non-carbon elements like phosphorus, chlorine, or metal hydroxides, act primarily through heat absorption and barrier formation. Both types are crucial for improving battery safety and performance in various applications, including electric vehicles and consumer electronics.</p></p>
