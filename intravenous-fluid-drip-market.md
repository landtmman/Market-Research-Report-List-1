<p><p>The intravenous fluid drip market is primarily segmented into two types: macro drip and micro drip. Macro drip sets are designed for administering large volumes of fluids quickly, typically delivering more than 20 drops per milliliter, making them suitable for emergency situations and rapid resuscitation. In contrast, micro drip sets deliver smaller volumes, usually between 60 drops per milliliter, allowing for precise control of fluid intake, which is essential in pediatric or critical care settings. Both types cater to specific patient needs and clinical situations.</p></p>
