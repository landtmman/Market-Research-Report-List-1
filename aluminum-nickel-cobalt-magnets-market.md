<p><p>The Aluminum-Nickel-Cobalt (AlNiCo) magnets market is primarily divided into two types: cast and sintered. Cast AlNiCo magnets are produced by pouring molten alloy into a mold, resulting in complex shapes with excellent magnetic properties. They offer high resistance to demagnetization. Sintered AlNiCo magnets, on the other hand, are made by compacting powdered alloy and subjecting it to high temperatures, leading to denser magnets with improved magnetic strength and stability. Both types find applications in various industries, including electronics and automotive.</p></p>
