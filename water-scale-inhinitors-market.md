<p><p>The water scale inhibitors market is primarily divided into two types: inorganic and organic. Inorganic inhibitors, such as phosphates and silicates, prevent scale formation through mechanisms like crystallization and sludge modification. They are often used in industrial applications due to their effectiveness at high temperatures. Organic inhibitors, including polyacrylic acids and phosphonates, function by sequestering calcium and other minerals, thus preventing deposits. These are commonly used in residential and commercial water treatment systems, offering flexibility and environmental safety.</p></p>
